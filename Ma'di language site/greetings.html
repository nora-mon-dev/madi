Greetings (Lazi) 👋🏿

Lazi is the Ma’di word for "greeting." You’ll often hear:

"Ma zi anyi ra" – I have greeted you all

Ingoni, Ingoni ku, Ma cui

The most common way to say “hi” or “hello” in Ma’di is:

  • Ingoni – How? (used like "Hello")
  • Ingoni ku – How, no (used like "I’m good" in response)

⚠️ Note: Ingoni ku should only be used as a response to Ingoni. It doesn’t work as a response to nye ingoni ("How are you?").

When asked 'Nye ingoni' (How are you), you can reply with 'Ma cui' meaning 'I am good/well'

Common greeting - translations

English Ma'di
Hello? Ingoni?
I am good (in response to ingoni) Ingoni ku
I am good/well Ma cui
What's the problem? Lejo adu?
No problem or issues Lejo iyo
Good morning Owira
Good afternoon/evening Ebi nyiraa

Nyi paranga ra
Good bye Adri endre
