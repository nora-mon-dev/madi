Ma'di ti (Ma'di language)

Ma’di ti is a central Sudanic language spoken in South Sudan and Uganda by the Ma’di people. There are several Ma’di dialects, however the 3 most common are:

  • Ma'di Lokai (South Sudan)
  • Ma'di Uganda
  • Ma'di Burulo

The site focuses on Lokai Ma’di, a Southern Ma’di dialect.

Tone

Ma’di is a tonal language, meaning the pitch used when pronouncing a word can change its meaning. There are three tones: high, medium, and low.

For example, the word Ma’di can take on different meanings depending on the tone:

English word Ma'di word English sentence Ma'di sentence
Ma’di language Ma’di ti I speak the madi language Ma madi ti jo awo
Person Madi I’m a good person Ma madi cui ri i
I'm here Ma di Look, i’m here Nye dre, ma di
My hand Ma drii My hand hurts Ma drii kaza
Ma dri Mine The ball is mine Mopira nai madri i

Please note: Some Ma’di speakers use diacritical marks when writing Ma'di ti to indicate different tones between words. For simplicity and ease of understanding, this site does not include diacritical marks in the written words.

Alphabets

The Ma’di alphabet follows a similar pattern to the English alphabets. However you’ll notice the letters Q, T are missing because these letters do not exist in Ma’di ti.
