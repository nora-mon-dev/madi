Family (Kaka) 👨🏿 👩🏿 👧🏿 👦🏿

Family in Ma’di culture extends beyond the nuclear unit commonly seen in the Western world. For example, aunties and uncles may be called mum and dad, even though there are specific words for each relative. This reflects the communal and relational nature of Ma’di identity.

Clan

The word Suru can mean clan or tribe. While some Ma’di may say “my Suru is Ma’di,” it's important to note that there are many sub-clans within the Ma’di people.

Aunties & Uncles

Ma’di has distinct words for aunties and uncles depending on whether they are from the mother’s or father’s side:
  • Mum’s brother - Adrua
  • Mum’s sister - Enderi
  • Dad’s brother - Atari
  • Dad’s sister - A’u

    • ⚠️ Enderi comes from the word for mother which is 'endre' and atari comes from the word for father which is 'ata'
